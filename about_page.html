Bradley, His Book

About Bradley, His Book

Beyond its earnest title, Bradley, His Book was Will H. Bradley’s 1896 periodical featuring self-made poems, short stories, artwork and advertisements. Bradley is sometimes considered the first American Graphic Designer and his pieces were a foundation for the American Art Nouveau. His talent and passion bleed through the pages. An inspiration to Dart regarding the scope of one’s work, Will H. Bradley was a man ahead of his time.

H.K. Frenzel and Gebrauchsgraphik

About Frenzel

Founded in the chaos of 1920s Germany, H.K. Frenzel created Gebrauchsgraphik with a clear and simple belief; advertising art was a force for good in the world. Regarding commercial art as ‘the great mediator between peoples, [and] the facilitator of world understanding,’ Frenzel’s idealism paved way for the design advancements of the 20th century. Stressing function over style, the journal and its founder hold a core tenant in Dart’s methodology and beliefs.

The Poster

About The Poster

Devoted to outdoor advertising and poster art, The Poster diverged from European criteria-based art towards strictly commercial works. This was art with a purpose; art for the sale. Featuring detailed penmanship, Dart views The Poster as an icon beyond its 1939 closure because anyone can understand what the features are selling, and all can appreciate the looks.

Design Quarterly

About Design Quarterly

Designed in 1946 to supplement the Walker Art Center’s display of functional objects, this Minneapolis publication’s ideals answered the question “Is there art in a broomstick?” with a resounding “... if it is designed for both usefulness and good looks.” This cutting edge publication continued its pioneering inquiry into functionality with the 1986 piece “Does it Make Sense” (a poster erupting from the pages to highlight computer-made graphic design). The poster’s divergence from the typical magazine page was an allegory for the digital art and lifestyle we experience today.

1960s Marvel Comics

About Marvel

Elegant prose? Missing. Detailed Graphics? Absent. The virtue in the early works of Stan Lee and Jack Kirby clue us into the power in the simplicities of communication. With a limited color spectrum and little space for dialogue Marvel managed to illuminate the imaginations of countless readers with its fluid motion and heroic themes. Dart is happy to see how the cheezy publications of the 1960’s have evolved into a staple of modernity.

Novum Gebrauchsgraphik

About Novum

A late 20th century evolution of Gebrauchsgraphik, Novum Gebrauchsgraphik incorporated the coming of post-modernism and the computerisation of design. Often regarded as subsidiary to the highly regarded Graphis, the publication displayed the works of less known artists from regions often excluded in design manuals. Eventually coming into their own mid-80s, the eye-catching and often abstract covers continue to impress Dart.

M.C. Escher

About Escher

A man whose work embodies the complexities of mathematics, Escher (a man who failed his high school exams) and his legacy would explore the nature of possibility. Often written off as an artist whose superb technique lacked artistic inspirations, Dart finds the beauty of Escher to be within the union of simultaneous and different viewpoints.

Leif Podhajsky

About Podhajsky

Abstracting natural imagery into digital work, Leif Podhajsky is our most watched artist today. Upon looking at the cover art to Tame Impala’s 2010 record Innerspeaker, Dart was drawn to the detail in his work which can be viewed rather simply. The scope of Podhajsky’s work continues to impress, along with its originality.
